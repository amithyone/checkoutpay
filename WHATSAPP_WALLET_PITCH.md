# WhatsApp Wallet — The idea

**Peer-to-peer money, in WhatsApp, including across countries**

---

## The idea in one breath

People already **plan, trust, and chat** in WhatsApp. The wallet is the bet that **sending value** can work the same way: **one person on WhatsApp** sends money to **another person on WhatsApp**—**not** by sharing long bank details in a group, but as a **direct, person-to-person** move of balance, with the **right amount in the right currency** on each side when they’re in **different countries**.

---

## WhatsApp to WhatsApp (P2P)

- The **sender** and **receiver** are both just **phone numbers in WhatsApp**—the same identity people use to message every day.
- A **peer-to-peer** transaction means: **I choose you** (your number / your wallet), **I choose an amount**, **you get credited**—like handing cash across a table, except the “table” is **WhatsApp** and the limits are whatever the product sets (caps, verification, etc.).
- No separate “download another app to pay me” step for the core story: the **idea** is **pay the person you’re already talking to**.

---

## Cross-country: why it’s different

When both people use the **same currency**, P2P is easy to explain: **100 in, 100 out** (minus any fees you choose to show).

When they’re in **different countries**, they usually mean **different currencies** (for example **naira** vs **Namibian dollars**, or **cedis** vs **pounds**). Then a straight “send 100” is ambiguous: **100 of what?**

The idea of **cross-country conversion** in this product is:

1. **Sender** pays in **their** currency—the amount they understand (what leaves their wallet).
2. **Receiver** gets credited in **their** currency—the amount that **lands** in their wallet.
3. **Transparency**: before they confirm, both sides should see something like **“You pay X / They receive Y”** and a **fair, understandable exchange** (rate or implied rate), so **P2P across borders** feels as clear as **P2P next door**.

That’s the product concept: **not** “hidden FX in the fine print,” but **conversion as part of the peer-to-peer deal**—**you see what cross the border actually means** in money terms.

---

## Why this matters (the problem space)

- **Remittance** and **informal transfers** often hop through cash, agents, or apps people don’t trust.
- **WhatsApp** is already where families and small businesses **coordinate**. Putting **wallet-to-wallet P2P** there reduces **mental switching** between “where we talk” and “where we pay.”
- **Cross-border** among everyday users is often **opaque**. The idea is to make **conversion explicit** so **peer-to-peer** stays **peer-to-peer**: two people, two amounts (if two currencies), **one clear transaction**.

---

## What we’re not describing here

This note is **only the idea**: **WhatsApp ↔ WhatsApp**, **P2P**, **cross-country conversion**. It does **not** cover **how** the system is built, which servers, or which integrations—those belong in engineering docs, not in the pitch.
