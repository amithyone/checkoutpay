Important: This Privacy Policy is provided for transparency about how CheckoutPay handles information. It is not legal advice. Businesses should obtain independent counsel regarding their own compliance obligations (including Nigeria Data Protection Act requirements where applicable).

This Privacy Policy describes how CheckoutPay ("we", "us", "our") processes personal data in connection with services offered through check-outpay.com and related applications (the "Services").

1. Introduction and scope

This Policy applies to visitors, registered merchants, consumers using wallet features, developer partners, and individuals whose data is processed because they pay a merchant using CheckoutPay.

It should be read with our Terms and Conditions and Security page.

2. Who we are and regulatory context

CheckoutPay provides payment and business tools for organisations operating in Nigeria. Certain regulated payment, settlement, and safeguarding functions are delivered in cooperation with MetroOven Innovations, operating within applicable Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) and Nigeria Deposit Insurance Corporation (NDIC) frameworks as relevant to its licence and role.

For privacy requests, use the contact methods at the end of this Policy.

3. Roles: merchant, consumer, developer partner

You acknowledge that operational details, thresholds, and enablement criteria may vary by account type, verification status, and product module without prior notice where permitted by law.

Merchants are businesses that accept payments through the platform. Consumers may use wallet or checkout experiences. Developer partners are integrators participating in our developer program where offered. Each role may involve different categories of data.

4. Categories of personal data

Depending on your relationship with us, we may process identifiers, contact details, financial and transaction information, device and log data, communications, and documents submitted for verification.

We collect what is reasonably necessary—we do not maintain a public inventory of every internal database field.

5. Account and profile data

Business registration may include legal name, trading name, address, phone, email, tax or registration identifiers, website URLs, and user accounts for staff.

We may record login timestamps, IP addresses, and security events associated with accounts.

6. Payment and transaction data

We do not describe internal matching or reconciliation mechanics on this page.
